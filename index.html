Welcome

This site is a reference for the faithful seeking to understand traditional Catholic doctrine on indulgences, sin, and commonly misunderstood teachings of the Church. All content draws from the perennial Magisterium, the Council of Trent, the Roman Catechism, papal encyclicals, and the Summa Theologica of St. Thomas Aquinas.

"The truth is like a lion; you don't have to defend it. Let it loose; it will defend itself." — St. Augustine

How to Use This Site

Use the navigation sidebar to explore the three major sections:

  • Indulgences — The Church's treasury of merit, conditions for gaining indulgences, and the most common indulgenced prayers with their texts.
  • Types of Sin — A thorough examination of mortal and venial sin, organized by the Ten Commandments and the Seven Deadly Sins.
  • Church Teachings Clarified — Clear explanations of doctrines that are often misunderstood or misrepresented, from the Real Presence to Papal Infallibility.

A Note on Sources

Throughout these pages, you will find references to:

  • The Council of Trent (1545–1563) — the great reforming Council that clarified Catholic doctrine
  • The Roman Catechism (Catechism of the Council of Trent, 1566)
  • The Summa Theologica of St. Thomas Aquinas
  • Various papal encyclicals and documents of the Holy See
  • The 1917 Code of Canon Law
  • The Raccolta — the official collection of indulgenced prayers and works
